A World Without Art

May 15, 2000

To the Editor:

If information pirates like Ian Clarke are allowed to have their way and destroy the copyright system (front page, May 10), what they will be left with is a new world bereft of any worthwhile art. What Mr. Clarke and others like him forget is that it is terribly expensive to create art, be it film, music or literature.

If an artist (or the artist's financial supporter) has no way to recover the costs of producing art, then what will be the incentive to create it? The cost of publishing a novel or recording a song is beyond the means of most people.

It is already difficult enough for independent artists to produce their art. Let's not shut them down for good.
